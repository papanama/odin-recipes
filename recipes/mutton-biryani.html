Mutton Biryani

Home

Mutton biryani is a layered rice dish cooked with marinated mutton(goat meat), long-grain basmati rice (or other suitable rice variety), and a blend of aromatic spices. The dish is typically slow-cooked in a sealed pot in a method called "dum-style", which allows the flavors to meld beautifully and the meat to become tender.

Ingredients

  • Mutton
  • Basmati Rice
  • Spices
    • Cardamom
    • Cinnamon
    • Cloves
    • Bay Leaves
    • Star Anise
    • Ginger
    • Garlic
    • Cumin
  • Yogurt(Curd)
  • Herbs
    • Mint
    • Coriander
  • Ghee/Oil
  • Safforn (Optional)

Instructions

  1. Marination: Mutton pieces are marinated with yogurt, spices, herbs, and lemon juice.
  2. Rice Preparation: Basmati rice is washed and soaked, then partially cooked with whole spices until al dente (firm to the bite).
  3. Meat Preparation: The marinated mutton is cooked until tender, either in a pressure cooker or a heavy-bottomed pot.
  4. Layering: The cooked mutton and par-cooked rice are layered in a pot, interspersed with fried onions, herbs, and saffron milk (if using).
  5. Dum Cooking: The pot is sealed tightly and slow-cooked over a low flame, allowing the flavors to penetrate the rice and meat.
