Samosa

Home

Samosa is a popular Indian snack made of a crispy pastry filled with a savory mixture of spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. They are typically deep-fried until golden brown and served with chutney or sauce. Samosas are often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer and are a staple in Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (for the pastry)
  • Potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • Green peas (optional)
  • Spices
    • Cumin seeds
    • Coriander powder
    • Garam masala
    • Red chili powder
    • Turmeric powder
  • Fresh cilantro (chopped)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dough: Mix all-purpose flour with a pinch of salt and enough water to form a smooth dough. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the filling: In a pan, heat some oil and add cumin seeds. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes, green peas, and spices. Cook until well combined and flavorful. Let it cool.
  3. Shape the samosas: Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval shape. Cut it in half to form two semicircles. Take one semicircle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edges. Fill the cone with the potato filling and seal the top edge.
  4. Fry the samosas: Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry the samosas in batches until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  5. Serve hot: Enjoy the samosas with mint chutney or tamarind sauce as a dipping option.

Enjoy your homemade samosas!
